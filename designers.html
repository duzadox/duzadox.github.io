DESIGNERS

Khoon Hooi

Khoon Hooi (full name Lee Khoon Hooi) is one of Malaysia’s top fashion designers who, since launching his eponymous label two decades ago, has won numerous awards, dressed celebrities and the well-heeled, established his own accessories line and, more recently, launched an exclusive jewellery collection with online diamond jeweller Zcova.

Inspired by his tailor grandmother, with whom he lived while growing up, and who instilled in him an appreciation for beautiful things, Khoon Hooi studied fashion design and graduated from the Malaysian Institute of Art. While working as an assistant designer for women’s fashion label Voir and department store Isetan, he regularly took part in fashion competitions both locally and abroad as a way to hone his skills, and won several awards along the way including the Asian Young Designer award in Japan.

In 1999, Khoon Hooi founded his self-named label, which became known for its intricate embroidery, high-quality printed fabrics, and feminine silhouettes. A regular participant at Paris Fashion Week and other international fashion trade shows, in 2020, the celebrated couturier and fashion designer achieved yet another milestone when Hollywood celebrities Ginnifer Goodwin, Karen Pittman, Katy Perry, and Zooey Deschanel were spotted in Khoon Hooi creations during awards season.

In the same year, the style guru made headlines as one of the first fashion designers who volunteered to sew Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) for front-liners in the pandemic. He also created an accessories line, Fugeelah, for the namesake social enterprise in collaboration with his friend and Malaysian model Deborah Henry. Any profits from the accessories, which were made entirely from leftover fabrics, were channeled into supporting refugee education at the Fugee School.

Dato' Seri Farah Khan

Dato’ Seri Dr Farah Khan is the founder and president of Malaysia’s leading luxury retailer The Melium Group. As much a fashion icon as a highly-astute businesswoman, Dato' Seri Farah is well regarded for leading Malaysia’s transformation into a sophisticated and stylish Southeast Asian hub over the past 30 years, concurrently building a formidable retail empire.

The Melium Group is one of Malaysia’s designer retail powerhouses, having represented some of the most prestigious luxury fashion brands including Saint Laurent, Lanvin, Givenchy, Hugo Boss, Stuart Weitzman, Tod’s, Roger Vivier, Max Mara, Weekend Max Mara, Emilio Pucci, Aigner, MCM, and Hackett London. Today, the Group distributes more than 100 international luxury fashion brands through a network of 35 standalone and two mixed-brand stores. The Group is also credited for introducing coffee culture to Malaysia, operating today the largest network of Dome restaurants outside Australia.

In 2007, Dato' Seri Farah launched her eponymous fashion label, Farah Khan. The label’s DNA is centred around the heritage art of handstitched embellishments and has regularly graced the red carpet thanks to fans like Selena Gomez, and Gigi and Bella Hadid. In tandem with her work, Dato' Seri Farah has an enduring commitment to social responsibility. Over the course of the past 30 years, she has created and spearheaded numerous original fundraising initiatives, the most notable being The Melium Group’s 30th-anniversary auction in collaboration with Tatler Malaysia, which raised RM4.5 million that benefited 20 Malaysian charities and NGOs.

Bernard Chandran

Bernard Chandran was born in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia on February 27, 1968. Growing up in an Islamic country and coming from a mixed parentage background of Indian and Chinese, he was exposed to a rich understanding of cultures from a young age.

Initially studying accounting, he soon realized that his true passion lay in fashion design. In 1988, he made a career switch and enrolled himself in IFTC (International Fashion Training College), a local fashion college. Recognizing the need to further his career in fashion, he moved to Paris with the support of his then-girlfriend (now wife) and mother.

In 1993, Bernard Chandran graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Fine Arts from the Paris American Academy and Pattern Making at L'Union Des Chambers Syndicales Parisiennes, Paris. During his time in Paris, he achieved significant recognition, becoming the first non-European designer to win both the coveted Silk Cut Young Designers Award and the Open European contest for Look of the Year 2000 in 1991.

Paris inspired Bernard and deepened his understanding of his own culture. Upon graduating, he returned to Malaysia with the goal of becoming the "Chanel of Malaysia." In 1994, he introduced a new silhouette and innovative techniques using embellishments and fabrics in traditional Malay outfits, revolutionizing their design and wear. His talent gained recognition among aristocrats in Malaysia and even reached international celebrities such as Michelle Yeoh, Lady Gaga, Estelle, Tori Amos, and many others.

Bernard Chandran continues to inspire young designers in Malaysia, paving the way for aspiring individuals in a conservative fashion landscape. His influence and talent have earned him numerous accolades, including being conferred the title of Dato (the Malaysian equivalent of a knighthood) by the Sultan of Pahang in 2007 for his outstanding contributions to fashion design. He was even invited to hold a solo exhibition at the prestigious Sultan Abu Bakar Museum, an old palace turned Museum.
